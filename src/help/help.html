LLM Policy Guardian Help

Getting Started

LLM Policy Guardian helps ensure that prompts sent to AI language models comply with your company's ethical and usage policies. It monitors interactions with major LLM platforms and provides warnings or blocks potentially problematic prompts.

Supported Platforms

  • ChatGPT (chat.openai.com)
  • Microsoft Copilot (copilot.microsoft.com)
  • Anthropic Claude (claude.ai)
  • GitHub Copilot (github.com)

How It Works

When you submit a prompt to an LLM, the extension:

  1. Intercepts the prompt before it's sent
  2. Checks it against your configured policy rules
  3. Either allows the prompt, displays a warning, or blocks the submission based on your settings

Key Features

  • Real-time Policy Checking: Ensures compliance with company policies before prompts are sent
  • Customizable Rules: Configure which policies to enforce and their severity levels
  • Detailed Logging: Keep records of policy violations for compliance and auditing
  • User-friendly Warnings: Provides clear explanations when policies are violated

Policy Rules

The extension includes several pre-configured policy rules:

Sensitive Data Protection

Detects and prevents sharing of personal identifiable information (PII), credentials, and API keys. This includes:

  • Email addresses
  • Credit card numbers
  • Social Security Numbers
  • API keys and passwords
  • Phone numbers
  • IP addresses

Proprietary Information

Prevents sharing of company confidential information and trade secrets, including:

  • Content marked as confidential or internal
  • Trade secrets
  • Internal code or systems
  • Revenue or sales data
  • Customer information

Prompt Injection Prevention

Detects attempts to manipulate LLM behavior through prompt engineering:

  • Instructions to ignore previous constraints
  • System prompt manipulation
  • Role-playing to bypass restrictions
  • Jailbreak attempts

Ethical Guidelines

Enforces company ethical guidelines for AI interaction:

  • Prevents requests for harmful content
  • Blocks illegal activity instructions
  • Identifies discriminatory content
  • Detects misinformation requests
  • Blocks harassment or privacy violations

Configuring the Extension

You can configure the extension through the Options page:

General Settings

  • Enable Extension: Turn the extension on or off entirely
  • Action Mode: Choose between "Warn Only" (allows override) or "Block Violations" (prevents submission)

Policy Rules

  • Rule Toggles: Enable or disable specific policy rules
  • Severity Levels: Set each rule's severity (Low, Medium, High)

Logging & Reporting

  • Enable Logging: Keep records of policy violations
  • Log Retention Period: How long to keep violation logs

Frequently Asked Questions

Will this extension send my prompts to a third party?

No. All policy checking happens locally in your browser. Your prompts are never sent to any external servers.

What happens when a policy violation is detected?

Depending on your settings, you'll either see a warning (which you can override) or the prompt will be blocked from submission.

Can I customize the policy rules?

The current version allows you to enable/disable rules and set their severity. Future versions will allow more customization of the specific patterns detected.

Where can I view violation logs?

Recent violations appear in the popup. For a full report, click the "Report" link in the popup footer.

How do I export my settings?

Go to the Options page and click the "Export Settings" button. This will download a JSON file with your current configuration.
